DATA 534 Group Project or Quiz 2

Wrap dat API!

The goal of this group project is to develop a R package wrapping a RESTful API.

You get to choose the API, and you are responsible for: the design and implementation of the package; the completeness and readibility of the documentations and of the vignettes; the test coverage of the code.

Bonus points are reserved for those project that decide to go public and open. Remember that to do that you DO NOT need a perfectly polished piece of software. You need a good idea, and that's pretty much the bare minimum. More about this in a moment.

You will work as a group, and I followed your suggestion to let you form the groups. However, this is not a democracy and you will have to report to me. To do that, I expect you to: (1) commit and push often (e.g., anytime you write some meaningful piece of code or documentation) and (2) keep a daily project notebook. I may not check every day, every person: I trust you.

Step 1 Form a group on Canvas with max 3 people

Step 2 Set up a group repo where your package will live, and work on it through pull requests (forks or branches, your choices) and NOT through direct commits. You will have a link to that repo and your project notebook in your own repo.

Step 3 Tell me what API you are going to work on, and what is, roughly speaking, your intended outcome. I may dissuade you from attempting something too ambitious (or in the really rare case that you are attempting something way too easy) but you are the boss. In general, projects fail because they are too ambitious, not because they are too easy.

Software requirements

rubric={code:25,mechanics:5}

The goal of the package is to wrap into a set of R functions a web REST API and offer a package for others to use those functions. The functions offered by the package should also take care of the minimum wrangling necessary to output the data in a viable format (that is, not as a raw binary file, unless a raw binary file is the most appropriate data format). You choose the API and the most appropriate input / output design of your functions. Think carefully of what part of the wrangling is of general interest (i.e., most or all of the users will want to perform it, and thus should be done in the package) and what part is only relevant as an example for the vignette (i.e., is too specific to be of general interest, and thus may just end up in the vignette.)

The package must be installable in Windows, Mac and Linux environments. Rare ecceptions are possibile, but you will need to extensively document and prove that you can't reasonably have your package to compile.

The functions in the code must be tested and documented. I'm talking not just forward facing documentation for the user, but also inline comments for developers to navigate your source code and understand it.

Read the Best practices for API packages httr vignette. Do it. Read them. I do expect your package to apply to the best practices. Also, recall what you learnt in Collaborative Software Development module).

Docs and vignettes

Special attention will be given to assess the completeness and readibility of your documentation and vignettes. Have at a least one vignette, illustrating most if not all of your package functionality. Documentation must be readable.
